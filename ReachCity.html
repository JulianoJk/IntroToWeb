How to Reach Athens

Athens, the capital city of Greece, is easily accessible by various means of transportation. Whether you're arriving by air, sea, or land, there are convenient options available to reach this historic city.

Athens International Airport, also known as Eleftherios Venizelos Airport (ATH), is the primary gateway to the city. It is located approximately 20 kilometers east of Athens city center. The airport serves numerous domestic and international flights, connecting Athens to major cities worldwide.

Athens is a major port city with several ports serving ferry routes from various destinations. The Port of Piraeus, the largest passenger port in Greece, is located approximately 10 kilometers southwest of Athens city center. It offers ferry connections to the Greek islands and other Mediterranean destinations.

Athens is well-connected to neighboring countries and cities by road and rail networks. The city is accessible by bus, train, and car from various parts of Greece and Europe. Highways and national roads provide easy access to Athens from major cities such as Thessaloniki, Patras, and Ioannina.
